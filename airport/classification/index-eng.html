Major AirportsThe largest airports that handle significant volumes of passengers and cargo. They have multiple runways, large terminals, and advanced infrastructure.Boryspil (Ukraine), Heathrow (London), Charles de Gaulle (Paris)- High passenger capacity.
- Developed infrastructure (hotels, shopping centers, parking).
- Serve both domestic and international flights.
Hub AirportsKey hubs for passenger transfers between different flights. They often have large terminals, convenient connections between flights, and advanced logistics systems.Dubai International (Dubai), Hamad International (Doha), Frankfurt (Frankfurt)- Specialize in transit passengers.
- Easy navigation between terminals.
- High number of flights from various airlines.
International AirportsAirports that serve international flights. They have special zones for international arrivals and departures, as well as border control and customs services.JFK (New York), Schiphol (Amsterdam), Incheon (Seoul)- Presence of customs and border control services.
- High number of international flights.
- Developed infrastructure for international passengers (lounges, duty-free shops).
Small and Local AirportsServe smaller cities and regions. They can be civilian, military, or combined. Typically, they have one runway, a limited number of flights, and small terminals.Ivano-Frankivsk (Ukraine), Lviv (Ukraine), Billund (Denmark)- Serve regional flights.
- Use smaller aircraft (turboprops, light jets).
- Limited infrastructure.
- Mostly domestic, but some may have a limited number of international flights.
